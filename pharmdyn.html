Pharmacokinetics (ADME)

Absorption: How the drug enters the bloodstream (PO, IV, Inhalation).

Distribution: Movement to tissues. Watch for Protein Binding!

Metabolism: Occurs in the Liver. Beware the First-Pass Effect!

Excretion: Elimination via the Kidneys. Monitor BUN/Creatinine.

Pharmacodynamics

  • Agonist: Mimics body response (e.g., Methadone).
  • Antagonist: Blocks response (e.g., Naloxone/Narcan).
  • Therapeutic Index: The ratio between effective and toxic dose. Small index = High Risk!

Safety Check: Dosage Devices

Nursing Consideration (Slide 17): NEVER use flatware spoons. Use calibrated spoons, oral syringes, or medicine droppers for accuracy.

NSAIDs (Ibuprofen)

Anti-inflammatory

Action: Inhibits COX-1/COX-2 enzymes.

Nursing: Take with food/milk to prevent GI ulcers.

Side Effects: GI bleeding (black stools), renal impairment.

Diphenhydramine (Benadryl)

1st Gen Antihistamine

Anticholinergic: Dry mouth, urinary retention, blurred vision.

Nursing: Fall risk! Caution in elderly (Beers Criteria).

Note: Crosses Blood-Brain barrier (sedation).

Bismuth (Pepto-Bismol)

Antidiarrheal / GI

Expected Findings: Black stools and black tongue (harmless).

Contraindication: Children with viral infections (Reye's Syndrome risk).

Oxycodone (Opioid)

RESPIRATORY DEPRESSION RISK

  • Monitor Respiratory Rate (Hold if <12).
  • Always assess level of sedation (S).
  • Implement bowel regimen (Stool softeners).
  • Have Naloxone (Narcan) available at all times.

Lisinopril (ACE Inhibitor)

  • Primary Use: HTN, Heart Failure.
  • Adverse Effect: Dry, persistent cough.
  • Life Threatening: Angioedema (Swelling of face/airway).
  • Monitoring: Watch for Hyperkalemia (High Potassium).
  • Education: Rise slowly (Orthostatic Hypotension).
