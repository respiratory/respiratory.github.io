Pathophysiology: The Battleground

Gram-Positive

  • Cell Wall: Thick peptidoglycan layer.
  • Shape: Cocci (circles) or Bacilli (rods).
  • Pathogen Example: Staph aureus.
  • Risks: Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS).

Gram-Negative

  • Cell Wall: Double membrane with Lipopolysaccharides (LPS).
  • Behavior: Triggers strong immune responses.
  • Complexity: Often forms biofilms and antibiotic resistance.
  • Risks: Sepsis, SIRS, and Endotoxic Shock.

Sepsis Alert: Both types trigger inflammatory responses that can progress to Sepsis/SIRS if the body can't contain the infection.

Pharmacology Spotlight

Tetracyclines

Ex: Doxycycline, Minocycline

Blocks 30S ribosome. Treats acne, STIs, malaria prophylaxis.

NURSING RED FLAGS:
Avoid milk/antacids. No pregnancy. Causes photosensitivity. Teeth staining in children < 8 yrs.

Aminoglycosides

Ex: Gentamicin

Broad Gram-neg. post-antibiotic effect. Treats sepsis and UTIs.

NURSING RED FLAGS:
Ototoxicity (Hearing) and Nephrotoxicity (Kidney). Requires Peaks & Troughs.

Beta-Lactams

Ex: Penicillins, Cephalosporins, Carbapenems

Inhibits cell wall synthesis. Broad utility.

NURSING RED FLAGS:
High Allergy/Anaphylaxis risk. Cross-sensitivity between Penicillin/Ceph. Carbapenems may cause Seizures.

Fluoroquinolones

Ex: Ciprofloxacin

Targets DNA replication. Excellent lung/prostate penetration.

NURSING RED FLAGS:
Boxed Warning: Tendon Rupture and Tendinitis. Prolongs QT interval. Avoid in children.

Glycopeptides

Ex: Vancomycin

The "MRSA Drug." Inhibits cell wall synthesis.

NURSING RED FLAGS:
"Red Man Syndrome" if infused too fast (hypotension). Ototoxic and Nephrotoxic.

Nitroimidazoles

Ex: Metronidazole (Flagyl)

Treats anaerobic bacteria and protozoa (Giardia, C-diff).

NURSING RED FLAGS:
NO ALCOHOL (Disulfiram-like reaction). Metallic taste is common. Increases Warfarin effect (INR).
