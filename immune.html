Nursing Guide to Vaccines & Immunomodulators

The Immune Branches

Innate

Primary Defense: Skin, Cilia, Stomach Acid. Non-specific and immediate.

Adaptive

B-Cells & T-Cells. Produces specific antibodies. Creates memory.

Immunity Types

  • Active: Body produces own antibodies (Vaccine or Infection). Long-term.
  • Passive: Transferred from another source (Breastmilk or Antivenom). Temporary.

Vaccine Classes

Live-Attenuated: MMR, Varicella. Avoid in Immunocompromised/Pregnancy!

Inactivated: Hep A, IPV, Hib.

mRNA: COVID-19.

Admin Locations

  • Infants: Vastus Lateralis (Thigh).
  • Older Kids/Adults: Deltoid (Arm).

Check Slide 7 for deltoid anatomy visual.

Admin Alerts

Contraindication: Moderate-to-severe acute illness. (Don't confuse the diagnosis!)

Side Effects: Localized redness/swelling. Orally = nausea. Intranasal = runny nose.

Herpes Zoster (Shingles) Quick-Fact

Pathophysiology: Reactivation of VZV dormant in sensory dorsal root ganglia.

EXAM PEARL: Pain often precedes the rash!

Risk Factors: Age > 50, Immune suppression, Stress.
Treatment: Antivirals (Acyclovir) must start within 72 hours.

Note: Rash is unilateral and follow a single dermatome (doesn't cross midline).

Immunosuppressants

Used to prevent organ rejection and manage Autoimmune disease.

Nursing Points:
  • High infection risk - Monitor subtle temp shifts.
  • NO LIVE VACCINES while on therapy.
  • Monitor kidneys/liver labs.

Prednisone (Glucocorticoid)

The Cushingoid Checklist:

Moon Face & Buffalo Hump

Hyperglycemia (Check Glucose!)

Purple Striae & Thin Skin

Osteoporosis


SAFETY: DO NOT STOP ABRUPTLY. Taper to prevent adrenal crisis.

Antihistamines (Benadryl)

Mechanism: Binds H1 receptors.

Side Effects: Drowsiness (crosses blood-brain barrier), dry mouth, urinary retention.

Caution: Pre-existing BPH or Glaucoma (Anticholinergic effects).
